Back


By Maddy Ipema


Every nightmare I have starts out as a dream

At first, I always seem to find myself

Cradling and craving the ocean blue

If I could have a beach day everyday

I would, but it wouldn’t like yours

No raunchy romance novel tucked

Under banana boat, white claws

Clinking together in the tote like

Lobsters in a tank

You would not find me

Basking my pale skin in the sun

My skin will find the sun

Organically as it is meant to

Without summoning or pleading

The island girl in me coming

Through like a medium

I will leave the beach a shade

No one can quite describe

And I like it that way

I will not be tangled up in some

Boy’s arms like a ship

Who has strayed far too away from shore

My lips will be smothered in sun spots

Kissing the edge of a spliff

My own hands will weave and wrap

Around my thimble, fragile self

I will expose my top

But never the bottom

Bearing my open chest to the world

And dipping it into the pool’s of eyes

Surrounding me like an oar

I come here to think and release

It always starts out this way

I will look at the vast water

With jealous and envy

How it flows in complete harmony

How it never scolds itself for

its waves becoming too powerful and consuming

How it can be what it is without spending it’s time

Obsessed with what it is not

The questioning and the doubt

It just moves without reason or fault

Oblivious like a child

I release the projections and the lies I feed to myself

Like baby sea turtles groping blindly for the sea

Return the fables I’ve made up back to the earth

I been running to the shore

To whisper my sins and explorations of debauchery

My fears and my incantations like

Confession, communion

Go for a quick baptismal dip

In the shallow neck

Creep in and emerge from the underbelly

A new beast like a captive alligator

Renewal

in the bath as a kid

I would always stay in far too long

And when my skin got all crinkled and wrinkled

I thought I was growing old before my eyes

My dad would laugh and say

“It’s just alligator skin”

He is always on the prowl

In my dream this is where the mutation happens

A new dawn turned dark

The sun will start to go down, down

My skin will grow far too cold and impatient

I will pack up my mind and spirit

Double checking I have not forgotten

Anything before I make the long journey

Home

That’s when he always finds me

And I think

“I knew I was forgetting something”

And he will say

“How could you forget about me”

And I will say I have in many ways

Like I can no longer recall once I wake

What my father looks like

I mean I know the basics right?

The color of the hair and the eyes

That starchy white ghost skin

I cannot take you with me

So I start running bare feet

My tiny toes slapping the pavement

Like a beluga’s body meeting the water

Once more, I dive back up for air

You chase me and I run, run, run

I try to make myself small so you

Cant’ see me but you seem to always

Be doing that on your own

Besides this was to be my dream away from home

My family tree never been no safe house

You never catch me

It’s all about the chase for you

And for me the destination

I will come back to the beach day

Once more lucidly

Even though I know you may be a lingering sun shadow

stalking up alongside me

Painting different pictures and scary figurines

Into my head like sigils in the clouds

Every nightmare I have starts out as a dream

Try as I might, how I try to forget

Maddy Ipema is a poet, non fiction essayist, and music freelance writer who is currently based in Chicago. She is a recent graduate from Columbia College Chicago, where she earned her BA in Creative Writing . She is a writer who utilizes her craft as a means to articulate her search for clarity, truth, and healing. Her work is often centered around exploring themes of deception, sensuality, and a profound search for identity. A writer who maneuvers from one genre and form to the next - whatever shape her writing takes is one that is meant to convey a search for meaning, self autonomy, and liberation.
